Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug, an American agronomist, humanitarian, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate, has been called "the father of the Green Revolution" for his research and development of high-yielding varieties of wheat. His work in Mexico, India, and Pakistan in the 1960s and 1970s helped to avert widespread famine, saving hundreds of millions of lives.

Borlaug's contributions to agriculture were immense. He developed semi-dwarf wheat varieties that were resistant to disease and could produce significantly higher yields than traditional varieties. This led to a dramatic increase in wheat production in many developing countries, helping to improve food security and reduce poverty.

Beyond his scientific achievements, Borlaug was a passionate advocate for global food security and international cooperation. He received numerous awards and honors throughout his life, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970.
